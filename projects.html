Sports & Entertainment
Open Deck

TKO
Marketing
Strategy

TKO Group Holdings operates some of the world's most recognized live entertainment properties. The challenge: converting passionate event audiences into sustained brand relationships — and giving sponsors something that lives beyond broadcast inventory.

Industry
Sports Entertainment
Scope
Brand Strategy
Partnership Architecture
Fan Engagement
Type
Speculative Concept
Problem
Live sports properties operate on event cycles, leaving massive cultural equity dormant between shows. Sponsors get broadcast slots — not genuine audience integration.
Insight
Live sports audiences don't just attend — they inhabit culture. The space between events is where identity forms, purchases happen, and fan loyalty is built or lost.
Strategic Direction
Reframe TKO as a year-round cultural platform — not an event calendar. Anchor partnerships in audience identity, aligning sponsor integrations with fan ritual rather than airtime.
Activation System
  • Athlete-forward brand storytelling across owned + earned channels
  • Limited partner collab drops timed to event weeks
  • Community hype programming in top-10 DMAs pre-event
  • Experiential fan zones with built-in partner integration

New Balance
Global
Partnership

New Balance carries rare heritage equity in a market flooded with celebrity-first moves. The brief: build a global partnership architecture that scales international relevance without compromising the authenticity that defines the brand.

Industry
Global Sportswear
Scope
Partnership Framework
Cultural Positioning
International Brand
Type
Speculative Concept
Problem
Heritage brands face a tension between legacy equity and modern relevance. Global expansion requires authentic resonance in every market — not a US playbook translated outward.
Insight
NB's greatest asset isn't a silhouette — it's perceived authenticity. In a market of celebrity noise, NB's partnerships should amplify earned credibility, not dilute it.
Strategic Direction
Build a tiered global partnership architecture rooted in local community athletes before macro-influencer plays. Use storytelling — not endorsements — as the primary partnership currency.
Activation System
  • City-specific heritage collections co-created with local cultural leaders
  • "Made Local" content series — athlete origin stories per global market
  • Long-term grassroots sport sponsorship over one-time event buys
  • Partnership-first flagship stores as cultural venues, not retail floors

Streetwear & Experiential
Open Deck

NoFree
Marathon
× CMPLXCon

Streetwear brands and culture festivals rarely build partnerships that feel mutual. This concept repositions NoFreeMarathon from sponsor to co-author — with a narrative arc spanning pre-event, on-site, and post-event, anchored in shared identity.

Industry
Streetwear / Culture Events
Scope
Experiential Strategy
Partnership Design
Product Activation
Type
Speculative Concept
Problem
Streetwear brands at festivals end up as logos on a banner. Sponsorship placements fail to integrate into the authentic experiences that actually drive community loyalty.
Insight
The CMPLXCon audience doesn't consume streetwear — they speak it as a first language. A partnership built on shared identity creates genuine cultural demand, not manufactured hype.
Strategic Direction
Make NoFreeMarathon a co-author of the CMPLXCon '26 experience. Build a three-phase narrative arc — pre-event, on-site, post-event — where the brand is woven into the story, not placed beside it.
Activation System
  • Co-branded pre-event content series building authentic hype
  • Exclusive collab drop — available only at the event activation
  • Immersive on-site environment (not a booth — a world)
  • Post-event earned media through attendee content and storytelling
  • Year-round community touchpoints sustaining the relationship
