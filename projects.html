01
Sports & Entertainment

TKO
Marketing
Strategy

Reframing a global sports and entertainment property as a year-round cultural platform — with a partnership architecture built on fan identity, not broadcast inventory.

Context
A Global Property Running on Event Cycles

TKO Group Holdings — parent of WWE and UFC — commands two of the most culturally dominant live event brands in the world. But the brand architecture operates primarily around event windows, leaving significant cultural real estate unused between shows.

Problem
Fans Treated as Ticket Buyers, Not Community

Sponsorship and partnership infrastructure leans on broadcast inventory and logo placements. Fan identity — the deepest, most durable asset — isn't being activated as a partnership vehicle. Cultural authority is being left on the table.

Insight
Live Sports Is the Last Appointment Media

In a fragmented attention economy, live combat sports are one of the few remaining formats where audiences actively choose presence. That presence is a premium cultural asset — and the space between events is where identity is reinforced, not where it disappears.

Strategic Direction
Year-Round Cultural Platform Architecture

Reframe TKO's brand portfolio as a cultural platform with a continuous heartbeat — not an event calendar. Anchor all partnership frameworks in audience identity segmentation, aligning sponsors with fan ritual rather than broadcast buys.

Activation System
How the Strategy Comes to Life
  • Athlete-forward brand storytelling series distributed across owned and earned channels — sustaining cultural momentum between events
  • Limited-edition sponsor collab drops timed to event weeks — creating urgency and product-level partnership integration
  • Community-led hype programming in top-10 DMAs pre-event — building local cultural density before the show arrives
  • Experiential fan zones with brand integration built on authentic fandom, not logo saturation
  • Fan identity loyalty framework bridging WWE and UFC audience overlap — enabling cross-property partnership pitches

02
Global Sportswear

New Balance
Global Partnership
Strategy

A tiered international partnership framework that protects brand authenticity while scaling cultural relevance across global markets through athlete storytelling and community-first activations.

Context
Heritage Brand at Cultural Prime Time

New Balance has experienced a remarkable cultural resurgence — but momentum requires more than product drops. The brand risks being perceived as a trend rather than a permanent cultural institution, especially in international markets where equity is still being established.

Problem
Global Expansion Without Losing the Plot

Heritage sportswear brands face a tension between legacy equity and modern cultural relevance. Global expansion requires partnership frameworks that feel authentic in every market — not just translated from a US-first playbook.

Insight
Community Is the Distribution Channel

NB's greatest asset isn't a silhouette — it's perceived authenticity. In a market flooded with celebrity-first moves, consumers are gravitating toward brands that feel earned. NB's partnerships should amplify that earned credibility, not dilute it with macro-influencer buys.

Strategic Direction
Global Community Partnership Architecture

Build a tiered global partnership framework connecting NB with cultural communities in key international markets. Local athletes as brand ambassadors — not celebrities, but credible community figures. Co-created product storytelling rooted in place and people, not performance specs alone.

Activation System
How the Strategy Comes to Life
  • City-specific heritage collections co-designed with local cultural leaders in 8 global markets
  • "Made Local" content series surfacing athlete origin stories — long-form narrative built for earned distribution
  • Long-term grassroots sport community sponsorship replacing one-time event buys — investment, not placement
  • Partnership-first retail activations turning flagship stores into cultural venues, not just product showrooms
  • Community Ambassador Program — non-celebrity tier with market-by-market credibility mapping

03
Streetwear & Experiential

NoFreeMarathon
× CMPLXCon
'26

A multi-phase partnership framework where the brand becomes co-author of the festival experience — not a sponsor. Built around shared identity, limited product, and a narrative arc that lives beyond the event date.

Context
Culture Conventions Missing the Street

CMPLXCon is one of the premier sneaker and streetwear conventions — but the format risks becoming insular. Brands show up, set booths, leave. No lasting community artifact, no neighborhood integration, no reason for culture to keep talking after Sunday.

Problem
Sponsorship That Feels Transactional

Streetwear brands and cultural festivals struggle to create partnership structures that feel mutual rather than bought. Traditional sponsorship placements lack integration into the authentic experience that actually drives audience loyalty — they sit on top of it.

Insight
The Marathon Is a Cultural Frame, Not Just a Brand

NoFreeMarathon isn't just a brand — it's a philosophy: the grind, the journey, the refusal to wait for permission. That frame resonates deeply with streetwear culture's core values. Tying a live convention experience to that ethos creates something beyond merch — it creates meaning.

Strategic Direction
Co-Author the Experience, Don't Sponsor It

Position NoFreeMarathon as the cultural anchor partner of CMPLXCon '26 — not a sponsor, but a co-architect of the experience. Design immersive brand zones, limited-edition collaborative drops, and programming that extends beyond convention walls into the surrounding neighborhood.

Activation System
How the Strategy Comes to Life
  • The Marathon Zone — dedicated immersive floor footprint at CMPLXCon '26, designed as an environment, not a booth
  • Co-branded pre-event content series building hype and community dialogue in the 8 weeks before the show
  • Exclusive NFM × CMPLXCon '26 limited collab product — available only at the activation, creating genuine scarcity
  • Block activation outside the convention open to non-ticket holders — extending the experience into the neighborhood
  • Post-event documentary release across social platforms — the cultural artifact that keeps earning media after the event closes
